Objective
🏆
How to Win
Empty your hand first — or avoid being the last person holding cards
▼
First out wins. Be the first player to play all the cards in your hand. Call "UNO!" on your second-to-last card, then go out on your next turn.

Alternatively: last player still holding cards loses. Everyone else is out once they empty their hand — the lone survivor at the end takes the L.
Setup
🃏
Dealing
112 cards, 7 per player
▼
Each player draws a card — highest value is the dealer. Shuffle the deck. Deal 7 cards to each player. Place remaining cards face-down as the Draw Pile. Flip the top card to start the Discard Pile.

If the flipped card is a Wild or Wild Draw 4, return it and flip again.
Playing
▶️
Taking Your Turn
Match color, number, or symbol
▼
Play passes to the left. On your turn, play a card that matches the top card by color, number, or symbol — or play any Wild card.

If you can't play, draw one card from the Draw Pile. If that card is playable, you may play it immediately.
📢
Calling UNO!
Say it before your last card
▼
When you're down to one card, you must yell "UNO!" before the card touches the Discard Pile.

If another player catches you not calling it before the next player takes their turn, you must draw 4 cards.
🔄
Going Out
Play your last card to leave the round
▼
Play your last card to go out. Remember to call "UNO!" on the card before it — if you forget and get caught, you draw 4.

If the last card is a Draw 2 or Wild Draw 4, the next player still has to draw before the round ends. Once you're out, you're done, you've won — but you can keep playing until only one person is left holding cards.
Key Rules
⚠️
Wild Draw 4 — Challenge Rule
You can only play it when stuck
▼
You may only play Wild Draw 4 when you have no card matching the current color. If a player suspects illegal play, they can challenge — the accused shows their hand privately:
  • If guilty: they draw the 4 cards instead.
  • If innocent: the challenger draws 4 + 2 extra (6 total).
Only the player required to draw 4 may challenge.
🚫
Reneging
You can skip a playable card
▼
You may choose not to play a playable card and draw instead. If the drawn card is playable, you may play it immediately. You cannot play a card from your existing hand after drawing.
Special Modes
👥
2 Players
Reverse acts as Skip
▼
  • Reverse acts like a Skip — you play again.
  • Skip lets you play another card immediately.
  • Draw 2 / Wild Draw 4 — opponent draws, play returns to you.
💀
Last Player Loses
Survival mode variant
▼
Players drop out as they empty their hands. The game continues until only one player remains with cards — that person loses. Great for larger groups where the stakes feel higher the longer you're still in it.

UNO SPEED PACK
Add-On Pack — combine with Classic UNO. Cannot be played alone. Each "Speed Up" icon raises how many cards everyone must play per turn!
16 Cards · Ages 7+
⚡
Speed Levels: Normal play = Level 1 (play 1 card). Each Speed Up icon played raises the level by 1 — everyone must play that many cards on their next turn.
Speed Rules
▶▶
Speed Up Icon
Raises the speed by 1 level
▼
Every card with a Speed Up icon (▶▶) raises the speed by one level. Starting with the next player, everyone must play an extra card per turn.

Level 2 → play 2 cards. Level 3 → play 3. Level 4 → play 4. And so on.

When playing multiple cards, each card must match the previous one.
▶▶▶
Wild Double Speed Up
Raises speed by 2, choose color
▼
Raises the speed by 2 levels at once. You also get to choose the color that continues play.
⊘
Action Cards & Speed
Only apply if played last
▼
Action Cards (Draw 2, Skip, Reverse, etc.) only take effect if they are the last card played on your turn. If you play 3 cards and the first is an Action Card, the next player doesn't do the action.
❓
Can't Keep Up?
Play what you can, then draw
▼
If you can't play the required number of cards, play what you can and then draw cards equal to the current Speed Level.

Example: Speed is 4, you can only play 2 → play those 2, draw 4 cards. Speed resets to 1 for the next player.
