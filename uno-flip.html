UNO
FLIP!
2–10 Players 7+ Years 112 Cards
Rules
Cards
🔄

UNO Flip plays like classic UNO — but the deck has two sides. You start on the Light Side. When someone plays a Flip card, everything flips over — including the cards in your hand — and you're now playing the Dark Side, with much harsher penalties.

Setup
1

Make sure all cards face the same direction — Light Side cards one way, Dark Side cards the other (they share the same physical card).

2

Each player draws a card and reveals the Light Side. Highest number goes first (symbols count as zero). That player becomes the dealer.

3

Dealer shuffles and deals 7 cards to each player. Hold cards so the Dark Side faces opponents.

4

Place remaining deck Light Side face-down as the Draw Pile. Flip the top card face-up to start the Discard Pile.

5

If an Action Card starts the Discard Pile, see the Action Cards tab for its special starting rules.

How to Play
🃏
Your Turn
Core loop
›
Match the top card of the Discard Pile by number, color, or symbol. You can always play a Wild card. If you have no match, draw one card from the Draw Pile — if it plays, you may put it down immediately; otherwise, play passes to the next person.

You may also choose not to play a matching card from your hand. Draw one card as normal. If that drawn card plays, you may play it — but you cannot play any other card from your hand afterward.
🔀
The Flip
The game-changing mechanic
›
When a Flip card is played, everything flips:

1. Flip the Discard Pile (the bottom card is now the top).
2. Flip the Draw Pile.
3. Every player flips their hand to the other side.

The new side stays in play until another Flip card is played, which switches it back. Cards must always face the correct direction for the active side.
📢
UNO! & Going Out
Last card rule
›
When you're down to one card, shout "UNO!". If another player catches you before the next player's turn begins, you must draw two cards.

When you play your last card, the round is over — either first player out wins, or you can keep playing and last player holding cards loses.

If a Draw card (Draw One, Draw Five, Wild Draw Two, or Wild Draw Color) is played as your last card, the affected player must still draw those cards before the round ends.

If the Draw Pile runs out, reshuffle the Discard Pile (except the top card) to form a new Draw Pile and keep playing.
⚔️
Challenging Wild Draw Cards
Wild Draw 2 & Wild Draw Color
›
Wild Draw 2 and Wild Draw Color can only be played when you have no card matching the current color (matching numbers or symbols don't count).

To challenge: If you suspect an illegal play, call it before drawing. The player who played it shows their hand.

• Guilty — they draw the penalty instead of you.
• Innocent — you draw the penalty plus 2 extra cards.
Winning
🏆
How to Win
Empty your hand first — or avoid being the last person holding cards
›
First out wins. Be the first player to play all the cards in your hand. Call "UNO!" on your second-to-last card, then go out on your next turn.

Alternatively: last player still holding cards loses. Everyone else is out once they empty their hand — the lone survivor at the end takes the L.
Special Situations

2-Player Game

Reverse cards act as Skip cards — the other player loses their turn and you play again.

Action Card as First Card

Draw One / Draw Five: the first player draws the penalty and loses their turn. Reverse: dealer plays first, then play moves right. Skip: first player is skipped. Wild: first player to the left chooses the color. Wild Draw 2: return to deck, draw a new starting card.

Keeping Cards Oriented

When drawing cards, always add them to your hand in the correct orientation for the active side. E.g., if playing Light Side, drawn cards go in Light Side facing you. Players must also keep their hand visible to all other players at all times.
