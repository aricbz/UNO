UNO
Liar's
2–6 Players Ages 7+ 112 Cards
Basics
Cards
Challenging
🤥
The twist: More than half the deck is Liar's Cards — played face down. You can lie about what you played. Other players can call your bluff.
Objective
🏆
Be the first to get rid of all your cards — or avoid being the last one standing. If you get caught lying, you draw cards. If you're honest and someone challenges you anyway, they draw.
The Two Card Types
REGULAR
Always played

LIAR'S

Setup
1
Shuffle and deal 7 cards to each player.
2
Place the remaining cards face down — this is the Draw Pile.
3
Flip the top card of the Draw Pile face up to start the Discard Pile. If it's a Liar's Card, flip it over and draw the next one.
4
The player left of the dealer goes first. Play moves clockwise.
Your Turn
→
Try to play one card onto the Discard Pile. A card is playable if it matches the top card in Color, Number, or Symbol. If it's an Action Card, it also does something special.
→
If you can't play, draw one card from the Draw Pile. You may then play that card if it's playable — otherwise your turn ends.
Calling "UNO!"
⚠️
When you have 1 card left, yell "UNO!" If someone calls it on you before you do, you must draw 2 cards.
Playing a Liar's Card
🤥
Playing it honestly (it actually matches)
›
If your Liar's Card matches the top card on the Discard Pile, play it face down and say out loud what it is. You're telling the truth. Other players can still challenge you — if they do, and you were honest, they draw 1 card.
😈
Playing it dishonestly (bluffing)
›
If your Liar's Card doesn't match, play it face down and lie about what it is — claim it's any matching card (e.g. "Red 3"). Your lie must be believable: the card you claim must match the current top card in Color, Number, or Symbol. Other players can challenge you.
Special Number Rules
7
7's Swap — swap hands with anyone
›
When you play a 7, swap your entire hand with any other player of your choice. Play continues in current order.
0
0's Pass — everyone passes left
›
When a 0 is played, all players simultaneously pass their hand to the next player in the current direction of play.

For Colorblind Players
👁
Each card includes a shape symbol: ● = Red · ◆ = Yellow · ▼ = Green · ■ = Blue
🎯
Challenging is the heart of Liar's UNO. Any player can challenge immediately after a Liar's Card is played — before the next player's turn.
⏩
Once a Liar's Card is played, the next player can challenge before taking their own turn. If no one challenges, play continues — and the card's stated identity becomes permanent.
How to Challenge
1
A player plays a Liar's Card face down and announces what it is.
2
Any other player can say "I challenge!" before the next turn begins.
3
The card is revealed. The truth comes out.
Outcomes
😇
They were telling the truth
›
The challenger was wrong. The challenger draws 1 card. The original player keeps their lead — no penalty.
🤥
They were lying
›
Caught! The liar must take their card back and draw 1 card as a penalty. The challenge was correct — no penalty for the challenger.
No Challenge Window
⚠️
If no one challenges before the next player takes their turn, the action takes effect as stated — the lie stands. You said "Red 3" and no one challenged? It was a Red 3.
Strategy Tips
🧠
When to bluff
›
Bluff when you have few cards and need to dump one fast. Bluff when the claimed card is believable. Bluff when other players are distracted or have been challenged themselves recently.
🔍
When to challenge
›
Challenge when a player with few cards plays suspiciously. Challenge when the claimed card feels unlikely based on what's been played. Be careful — a wrong challenge costs you a card.
🤫
Keep a poker face
›
You can be challenged even when telling the truth. Stay calm whether you're bluffing or not — hesitation and relief are tells. Channel your inner poker player.
Wild Liar's Challenge Card
🃏
There are special Wild Liar's Challenge Cards in the deck. When you play one, you choose the color — and all other players may play any card regardless of color or number on their next turn, as normal. These always go face down.
